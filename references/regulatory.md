# Epistemic Calibration: Regulatory & Legal

Domain-specific rules for applying the Epistemic Layer Separator to
compliance, regulation, legal analysis, and policy interpretation.

---

## Critical Warning

**This skill does not make Claude a lawyer or compliance officer.**

Even with perfect epistemic separation, regulatory analysis from an LLM is:
- Limited to training data (regulations change)
- Unable to account for jurisdiction-specific interpretations and case law
- Not a substitute for qualified legal counsel

---

## What Counts as Established in Regulatory Contexts

✅ **Truly Established:**
- Direct quotes from regulatory text (cite article and version)
- Official guidance documents from regulators (EBA, ESMA, CNIL, etc.)
- Court rulings and regulatory decisions — when cited specifically

❌ **Not Established:**
- Interpretations of ambiguous regulatory language — these are inferences
- "Regulators typically view X as..." — inference from pattern, not binding
- Any claim about regulations post-training cutoff
- Statements about how a specific regulator would rule on a novel situation

---

## Fast-Moving Regulatory Areas (default all to 🔴)

- AI Act implementation timelines and obligations
- MiCAR / crypto asset regulation in the EU
- Open banking / PSD3 developments
- National transpositions of EU directives

---

## The Synthesis Rule

Every regulatory synthesis that informs a material decision should end with:

> ⚠️ **This analysis is based on Claude's training data and is not legal advice.
> Regulatory text and interpretations change. Verify with a qualified legal professional
> before making compliance decisions.**
